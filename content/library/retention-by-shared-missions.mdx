## Retention principle

People return when they feel needed, not when they are merely notified.
Shared missions create explicit roles and visible progress, which increases social
commitment and return behavior.

## Mission design criteria

- One clear objective with a hard deadline.
- Specific role assignments (lead, operator, reviewer).
- Public proof requirement for completion.
- Small reward tied to meaningful contribution.

## Example weekly mission

- Objective: improve response quality in clan discussions.
- Lead: chief delegate.
- Operator: two members propose improved response template.
- Reviewer: elder validates quality shift using rubric.

## Metrics to track

- Week-1 retention delta between mission participants and non-participants.
- Average comments per active member during mission week.
- Share of members completing at least one mission action.

## Common failure mode

If missions become generic checklists, engagement drops.
Keep missions tied to real clan objectives and publish outcomes every week.
