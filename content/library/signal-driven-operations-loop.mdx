## Why this loop works

Most teams fail at execution because they treat signals as ideas, not as queued actions.
The loop below forces each signal through triage, ownership, and proof.

## The five-step loop

1. Capture one weak signal from live operations.
2. Convert it into a testable hypothesis.
3. Assign a single owner and 72-hour deadline.
4. Ship a measurable intervention.
5. Publish proof and decision (scale, iterate, or stop).

## Weekly operating template

- Monday: collect top five signals from clan channels.
- Tuesday: choose two to run through rapid test design.
- Thursday: review proof, compare against baseline metrics.
- Friday: post the after-action summary and next-week carryover.

## What to measure

- Time from signal capture to first intervention.
- Intervention success rate.
- Number of repeated issues after corrective action.

If the same issue appears three weeks in a row, the loop is too shallow and needs
system-level correction, not another tactical patch.
